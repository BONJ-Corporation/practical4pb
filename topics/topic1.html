COMPUTING FOR COMMUNICATION

Information access:

Computer science plays a big role in information access. Online reference materials, such as Wikipedia (The most popular online encyclopedia), share vast amounts of information which promotes researches. The invention of E-book and online article means that we no longer need to have physical texts as all we need can be accessed on the internet and some are accessible for free.

Creativity and Self-Expression:

The existence of Social Media, such as Facebook and Instagram, contributes greatly to the self-expression and creativity. Digitial functions, for instance audio-recording and video-recording, can be shared to the internet where it can easily be viewed by a large audience. This is possible thanks to the share function that every Social has Media, which allows content to be shared on a third person's field from the same platform.

Collaboration:

Computer science has given us more opportunities on how we can communicate regardless of the distance between parties. learning management system, such as blackboard and moodle, have allowed students to access courses online. The additional function available from these learning management systems, such as live commenting and screen sharing, has maximised and made teaching easier.

Routing:

Routers are what allow people with different networks to communicate. Emails, photo, and calls are divided into data packets and they are sent through a less congested route to reduce data-trafficking.
